## The Power User Mindset

More important than any specific technique is the mindset:

### 1. Think in systems, not sessions
Each session builds on the last. Your CLAUDE.md improves. Your knowledge base grows. Your skills accumulate. You're building a system that gets better over time.

### 2. Automate the repeated
If you do something twice, consider a command. Three times, definitely a command. If Claude Code makes the same mistake twice, write a skill. Your automation layer grows from real friction.

### 3. Decompose before executing
Before saying "do the thing," think: what are the parts? What can run in parallel? What needs human judgment? The 30 seconds of decomposition saves 10 minutes of rework.

### 4. Manage context deliberately
Context is finite. Be intentional about what goes in. Use agents for exploration. Compact when it gets heavy. Fresh context = better results.

### 5. Invest in configuration
The 20 minutes writing a good CLAUDE.md saves hours of repeated explanations. The hour building a /deploy command saves days across the year. Front-load the investment.

## The 1% Compounding

You won't become a power user overnight. But every small improvement compounds:

- A good CLAUDE.md saves 5 minutes per session
- A custom command saves 3 minutes each time you use it
- A skill prevents a mistake that would cost 20 minutes to fix
- Context awareness keeps quality high, saving rework time

Over a month, these small improvements add up to hours. Over a year, they transform your productivity.
